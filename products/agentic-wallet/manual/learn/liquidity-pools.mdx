## What is a liquidity pool

A **liquidity pool** is a smart contract holding reserves of two (or more) tokens. Anyone can deposit tokens to become a **liquidity provider (LP)** and earn a share of the trading fees paid by swappers.

AMM pools maintain a constant relationship between token amounts (e.g., `x × y = k` in Uniswap v2). Every trade shifts the balance — buying token Y removes Y from the pool and adds X, changing the effective price.

## LP tokens

When you deposit into a pool, you receive **LP tokens** representing your proportional share of the pool's reserves:

- LP tokens accumulate fee income as trades happen.
- LP tokens can be redeemed at any time to withdraw the underlying tokens plus earned fees.
- In Uniswap v3, LP positions are represented as NFTs (ERC-721) rather than fungible LP tokens.
- LP tokens can often be staked in yield farms or gauge contracts to earn additional protocol emissions on top of trading fees.

## Concentrated liquidity (Uniswap v3)

Standard AMMs (Uniswap v2) spread liquidity across all possible prices from zero to infinity. Most of that liquidity is never used.

Uniswap v3 allows LPs to concentrate liquidity within a **price range**:

- Liquidity is only active and earns fees when the current price is within the range.
- When the price moves outside the range, the position holds only one token and earns no fees.
- Narrower ranges produce higher fee yield per unit of capital when in range, but require more active management.

For agents managing Uniswap v3 positions, this means:

1. **Monitor the price** relative to the position's tick range.
2. **Rebalance when out of range**: remove liquidity → calculate a new range around the current price → re-add.
3. **Collect fees periodically**: fees accumulate in the pool and must be claimed by calling `collect()`.

## Key functions: NonfungiblePositionManager

Uniswap v3 LP positions are managed through the `NonfungiblePositionManager` contract:

| Function | What it does |
|---|---|
| `mint(params)` | Create a new LP position; returns an NFT `tokenId` |
| `increaseLiquidity(params)` | Add liquidity to an existing position |
| `decreaseLiquidity(params)` | Remove some liquidity from a position |
| `collect(params)` | Collect accumulated fees |
| `burn(tokenId)` | Close a position entirely (burn the NFT) |

Before calling `mint` or `increaseLiquidity`, approve the `NonfungiblePositionManager` to spend both tokens (ERC-20 approve for each).

**Example: Collect fees from a position**

```python
from web3 import Web3
from eth_abi import encode

NFT_MANAGER = "0xNonfungiblePositionManager..."
TOKEN_ID = 12345  # your LP position NFT tokenId
MAX_UINT128 = 2**128 - 1

selector = Web3.keccak(
    text="collect((uint256,address,uint128,uint128))"
)[:4].hex()

collect_calldata = "0x" + selector + encode(
    ["(uint256,address,uint128,uint128)"],
    [[TOKEN_ID, wallet_address, MAX_UINT128, MAX_UINT128]]
).hex()

result = await client.call_contract(
    wallet_id=wallet_id,
    chain_id="BASE_ETH",
    contract_addr=NFT_MANAGER,
    calldata=collect_calldata,
)
```

## Impermanent loss

When you provide liquidity, you deposit a roughly 50/50 value split of two tokens. The AMM mechanism continuously rebalances as prices move — it sells the appreciating token and buys more of the depreciating one.

**Impermanent loss** is the difference between:
- Holding both tokens without providing LP (HODL)
- Providing LP and receiving the rebalanced composition back

| Price change of one token | Impermanent loss |
|---|---|
| ±25% | ~0.6% |
| ±50% | ~2.0% |
| ±100% (2×) | ~5.7% |
| ±200% (3×) | ~13.4% |
| ±400% (5×) | ~25.5% |

It is "impermanent" because if prices return to the entry level, the loss disappears. The loss is realized when you withdraw at a displaced price.

Fee income offsets impermanent loss. High-volume pairs with stable prices (stablecoin/stablecoin, ETH/stETH) generate fee revenue with minimal impermanent loss risk — making them popular LP targets for agents.

## Stable swap pools (Curve)

Curve uses a different invariant optimized for tokens that trade near 1:1 (stablecoins, liquid staking pairs):

```
A × Σx_i + D = A × D + D^(n+1) / (n^n × Πx_i)
```

This produces much less slippage for like-value swaps than the constant product formula. Curve pools are the standard for USDC/USDT/DAI swaps and stETH/ETH.

Key Curve functions: `exchange(i, j, dx, min_dy)` for swaps, `add_liquidity(amounts, min_mint_amount)` for deposits, `remove_liquidity(lp_amount, min_amounts)` for withdrawals.
