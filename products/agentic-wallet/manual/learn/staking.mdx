## Types of staking

**Staking** means locking or committing tokens in exchange for rewards. The mechanism and risk profile vary significantly by type:

| Type | What you lock | What you earn | Can be liquidated? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Network staking | Native token (ETH, SOL) | Validator rewards (issuance + fees) | No — but slashing risk exists |
| Liquid staking | Native token | Staking yield + liquid receipt token | No |
| Protocol staking | Governance tokens (CRV, AURA) | Protocol fees or emissions | No |
| LP gauge staking | LP tokens | Protocol emissions (on top of trading fees) | No |

## Liquid staking

**Liquid staking protocols** accept ETH (or SOL) and stake it with validators on behalf of users, returning a **liquid receipt token** that:

- Represents the staked position plus accrued yield.
- Can be freely transferred, swapped, or used as collateral in other DeFi protocols.
- Does not require waiting for unstaking queues to access value.

| Protocol | Chain | Input | Receipt token | Yield source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lido | `ETH` | ETH | stETH / wstETH | Ethereum consensus rewards |
| Rocket Pool | `ETH` | ETH | rETH | Ethereum consensus rewards |
| Jito | `SOL` | SOL | jitoSOL | Solana staking + MEV rewards |
| Marinade | `SOL` | SOL | mSOL | Solana staking rewards |

### stETH vs. wstETH

`stETH` is a **rebasing token** — its balance increases daily as staking rewards accrue. Your wallet shows more stETH tomorrow than today, even without any action.

`wstETH` (wrapped stETH) is the **non-rebasing version** — your balance stays constant, but the exchange rate between wstETH and stETH increases. Use `wstETH` in DeFi protocols that do not support rebasing tokens (most lending protocols, many DEX pools).

## Staking ETH via Lido

Lido's staking accepts native ETH directly (no ERC-20 approve needed):

```python
from web3 import Web3
from eth_abi import encode

LIDO = "0xLidoStakingAddress..."  # verify from docs.lido.fi

# submit(address referral) — send ETH as msg.value
selector = Web3.keccak(text="submit(address)")[:4].hex()
submit_calldata = "0x" + selector + encode(
    ["address"], ["0x0000000000000000000000000000000000000000"]
).hex()

# Note: native ETH value handling depends on SDK version — check client.call_contract() docs
result = await client.call_contract(
    wallet_id=wallet_id,
    chain_id="ETH",
    contract_addr=LIDO,
    calldata=submit_calldata,
)
print(f"Staked: {result['tx_id']}")
```

## LP gauge staking (Curve / Convex)

In Curve's ecosystem, LPs deposit into a pool and receive LP tokens, then stake those LP tokens in a **gauge** to earn CRV emissions. Convex Finance auto-stakes LP tokens in Curve gauges and distributes rewards as cvxCRV, making yield harvesting more gas-efficient.

The flow:
1. `approve(pool_address, amount)` — approve the Curve pool to take your tokens.
2. `add_liquidity([amount0, amount1], min_lp_out)` — deposit into the pool, receive LP tokens.
3. `approve(gauge_address, lp_amount)` — approve the gauge to take your LP tokens.
4. `deposit(lp_amount)` on the gauge — stake LP tokens for emissions.
5. `claim_rewards()` or `withdraw()` — harvest or exit.

## Staking risks

**Slashing** — Ethereum validators can be penalized (slashed) for misbehavior (double signing, prolonged downtime). Liquid staking protocols absorb this risk across a large validator set, but it is non-zero.

**Smart contract risk** — Liquid staking protocols have been exploited. Lido and Rocket Pool are widely audited, but always check the current audit status.

**Withdrawal queue** — Ethereum has a validator exit queue. Unstaking ETH from Lido may take hours to days depending on queue depth. Agents managing time-sensitive strategies must account for this.

**Depeg risk** — Liquid staking tokens trade on secondary markets. `stETH` and `rETH` can temporarily trade below their ETH parity, especially during market stress. Strategies that assume 1:1 parity (e.g., using stETH as ETH collateral) are exposed to this.

**Gauge token emissions inflation** — CRV and other governance tokens earned through gauge staking can decrease in value due to continuous inflation. Net yield depends on token price, not just emission rate.
