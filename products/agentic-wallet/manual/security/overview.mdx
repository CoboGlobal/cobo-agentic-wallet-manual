Your agent can hold crypto, execute trades, interact with DeFi protocols, and make payments — all autonomously. That's the point. But every operation passes through your pact before it reaches the blockchain. Here's how that works.

<Info>Built on 8 years protecting institutional crypto. $2B under custody. Zero breaches.</Info>

## Your rules can't be broken

Your agent operates the wallet but cannot modify the rules that govern it — even if compromised. This isn't a best practice. It's architectural.

The agent's operator credentials are structurally separated from governance credentials at the infrastructure level. The agent can submit transactions, but it cannot:

- Raise or modify spending limits
- Change which addresses, chains, or contracts are allowed
- Approve its own requests
- Access wallets it hasn't been connected to
- Escalate its own permissions

The rules you set in your pact are enforced by a different layer than the one your agent talks to. There is no API call, prompt injection, or agent behavior that can change them.

## Your limits can't be gamed

Every transaction is checked against your pact's budget rules before execution. The policy engine enforces:

- **Per-transaction caps** — no single transfer can exceed your limit
- **Rolling budgets** — cumulative limits over hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly windows using sliding-window counters with atomic reservation
- **Chain and token restrictions** — your agent can only transact on chains and with tokens you've approved
- **Contract and function allowlists** — limit which smart contracts and specific functions your agent can call
- **Address allowlists** — restrict destinations to addresses you've explicitly approved
- **Review thresholds** — operations above a defined value pause for your approval before execution

The most restrictive rule always wins. Limits compose — a transaction must satisfy every applicable rule to execute. No race conditions. No workarounds.

## Your authority can't be bypassed

Signing authority for your wallet is split across multiple independent parties using Multi-Party Computation (MPC). This is a mathematical guarantee, not a software promise.

After you pair the wallet with the Human App, two separate threshold groups control signing:

- **Agent + Cobo** — handles everyday transactions that stay within your pact. Your phone is not involved for routine operations.
- **You + Cobo** — required for high-value operations above your approval threshold, policy changes, and ownership actions. Enforced cryptographically.

No single party — not your agent, not Cobo, not you alone — can move funds unilaterally. Every transaction requires cooperation, and that cooperation only happens when your pact allows it.

## Every decision is on record

Every operation your agent attempts — allowed, paused, or blocked — is logged with a timestamp, the policy decision, and the full context. Nothing happens silently.

From the Human App, you can:
- See your agent's full transaction history at any time
- Review why an operation was blocked or held for approval
- Approve or reject transactions waiting for your review
- Query audit logs for compliance or debugging

[Activity and audit logs →](/products/agentic-wallet/manual/get-started/activity)

## Pause or revoke in one step

- **Pause** — suspends your agent immediately. Your pact terms are preserved. Unpausing restores access with no re-approval needed.
- **Revoke** — permanently terminates the pact. Your agent must submit a new pact and go through approval before it can act again.

Both take effect the moment you confirm. There is no delay or grace period.

## What's at risk if something goes wrong

Your agent can only access the one wallet you connected it to — not any other wallets, and not more than the balance in that wallet. Within that wallet, your agent can only act within the pact you set.

If your agent is compromised, the damage is bounded by your pact. Transactions above your approval threshold are blocked until you review them. Pausing your agent stops all new operations immediately.

The one thing a pact cannot undo is a transaction your agent already executed within its approved limits. Set your limits at a level you're comfortable with, and review your activity regularly.
