## Your wallet

Your wallet is an on-chain account that holds your crypto. It has one or more addresses — the identifiers you share when someone wants to send you funds. The wallet itself lives on the blockchain; Cobo Agentic Wallet manages the keys that control it.

Every new wallet starts on a testnet. Testnets are practice environments where tokens have no real value — a safe place to verify everything works before moving to mainnet with real funds. See [Supported chains](/developer/overview/supported-chains) for the full list.

## Your agent

Your agent is an AI system that operates the wallet on your behalf. Once the Cobo Agentic Wallet skill is installed, it can transfer tokens, call smart contracts, check balances, and respond to policy decisions — all without needing you to approve each step manually.

Each agent is connected to exactly one wallet. You can connect multiple agents — each manages its own separate wallet. Currently, one wallet supports one connected agent at a time.

Before you pair the wallet with the Human App, your agent is the owner and has no restrictions. After pairing, your agent becomes a delegate: it can only act within the rules you set.

## Pairing with the Human App

Pairing is the step that puts you personally in control of your wallet. When you pair:

- Your signing keys are generated on your device (phone) and never leave it
- Your agent becomes a delegate operating through pacts and owner-side guardrails
- Over-limit operations pause and wait for your sign-off before executing

Pairing is optional. Your agent can operate without it — useful for testing and early development. When you're ready to use real funds under your personal control, pairing is how you establish that.

See [Pair your agent](/wallet-owner/connect-agent-human-app) for the step-by-step process.

## Owner guardrails

After you pair the wallet with the Human App, the app lets you configure owner-side guardrails for spending and approvals. These controls work alongside your active pacts and determine when your phone needs to step in.

The main controls are:

| Control | What it does |
|---|---|
| Per-transaction limit | Your agent cannot send more than this in a single transaction |
| Daily limit | Total your agent can spend per day |
| Approval threshold | Operations above this amount pause for your approval |
| Allowed addresses | Your agent can only send to addresses you've approved |
| Allowed networks | Your agent can only transact on the chains you specify |

You set and update these controls in the Human App — in plain language, through the AI assistant. See [Set rules and limits](/wallet-owner/policies).

## Pacts

A pact is a structured delegation agreement between you and your agent. When your agent wants to take on a new task or needs new authority, it submits a pact for your approval.

A pact consists of five elements:
- **Intent** — the job the agent is being authorized to carry out
- **Execution plan** — a reviewable plan for how the agent intends to carry out that job
- **Permissions** — predefined operation scopes the agent is allowed to use (e.g., wallet read, transaction execution)
- **Policies** — budgets, approvals, allowlists, chain/token/contract constraints, and other guardrails
- **Completion conditions** — what triggers automatic completion and revocation (time elapsed, budget spent, job done)

You review and decide: approve, reject, or adjust the terms before approving. Once you approve, your agent operates within those exact boundaries. Every pact has an endgame — when a completion condition is met, access revokes automatically. No zombie permissions. No forgotten API keys.

See [Review and approve pacts](/wallet-owner/approve) for how to handle these.

## Approval threshold

If a transaction exceeds your approval threshold, it pauses automatically and waits for you to decide. Your agent cannot proceed until you act. You receive a push notification in the Human App; tap to approve or reject. If you don't act within one hour, the transaction expires and your agent is notified.

This is distinct from a hard limit: a hard limit blocks the transaction outright. The approval threshold just routes it to you for review.

See [Handle transactions](/wallet-owner/pending-ops) for the full flow.

## MPC key shares

Your wallet's signing authority is split across multiple independent parties using Multi-Party Computation (MPC). No single party holds a complete key. Moving funds requires cooperation from a threshold of key share holders — which means no single point of failure and no single point of attack.

After pairing, two separate groups of key shares control your wallet:

- **Your agent's group** — agent machine + Cobo. Used for everyday transactions within an approved pact. Your phone is not involved for routine operations.
- **Your group** — your phone + Cobo. Required for over-limit operations and ownership actions. Enforced at the cryptographic level, not just by software.

See [MPC key shares](/security/mpc-key-shares) for a full explanation.

## Human App

The Human App is your primary management interface. It runs on your phone, and your key share is stored on the device. You use it to pair your agent, set rules, review and approve requests, monitor activity, and freeze or revoke your agent's access.

See [The Human App](/wallet-owner/human-app) for a full feature walkthrough.
