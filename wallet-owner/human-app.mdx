The Human App is the primary interface for wallet owners. After you claim a wallet, your signing keys are generated and stored on your device — they never leave it. All owner actions — approving your agent's requests, adjusting spending rules, reviewing activity, and freezing your agent — happen here.

## What you can do

| Task | How |
|---|---|
| Claim your wallet | Enter a claim code from your agent to take ownership |
| Approve authorization requests | Review and approve or reject requests your agent submits for new tasks |
| Approve over-limit transactions | Receive a push notification when your agent needs your sign-off; tap to approve or reject |
| Manage your Default Policy | Set spending limits that control what your agent can do automatically |
| Chat with the AI assistant | Ask about your balance, activity, or policies — or modify your rules in plain language |
| Freeze or revoke your agent | Pause or permanently remove your agent's access |
| Review activity | See your full transaction history and agent actions |
| Back up your key share | Protect your mobile key share with iCloud, Google Drive, or a file export |

## How approval works

When your agent submits a transaction that exceeds your Default Policy limit:

1. You receive a push notification on your phone
2. Open the Human App and review the transaction details — amount, destination, and the agent's stated reason
3. Tap **Approve** or **Reject**
4. Your device signs the transaction (or declines it) and the agent proceeds accordingly

The same process applies to authorization requests: when your agent wants to take on a new task outside its current permissions, it submits a request you review and approve or reject here.

## How your keys are protected

When you claim a wallet, the Human App generates a key share on your device. This share is part of a 2-of-2 MPC group: your device + Cobo. Neither party can move funds alone. Your agent has its own separate 2-of-2 group (agent machine + Cobo) for everyday signing — the two groups are independent, so your agent can transact within its policy without involving your phone.

Over-limit operations require your device to participate — this is enforced at the cryptographic level, not just by software rules.

See [MPC Key Shares](/security/mpc-key-shares) for a full explanation.
